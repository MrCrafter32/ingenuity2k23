Holi

Life is all about colors. Colors can serve as metaphors for the emotions we express. The combination of the seven primary colors to create a RAINBOW draws us in even more. If the sight of a distant rainbows’ brilliant colors is enough to make us happy, imagine how much more so we'd be thrilled to actually feel them.

The festival of colors, HOLI, is always ahead of the curve when it comes to filling people's hearts with joy and love through the universal language of hues. Celebrations like these are truly unique because they unite communities and stir a wide range of feelings simultaneously. The beauty behind coloring each other lies in every touch of color on your skin.

The coming together of people is in itself a cause for celebration, and it need not be in conjunction with any particular event or location. Especially in this tech world, where virtual greetings are on the upper hand, festivals like Holi, Diwali, etc. teach us what is meaning of happiness in togetherness.The festival of Holi is celebrated in many different ways and has many different origin stories across India, but its underlying message is always the same: good ultimately triumphs over evil. Holi is a festival honoring the arrival of spring as well as love and new beginnings.

Lohri

“Gajak mungfali di mithaas zindagi mein
Lohri ki aapko lakh lakh vadhaiyan!”

Lohri is a popular winter festival confined to the country’s northern parts. It is celebrated enthusiastically and traditionally on the 13th of January every year, marking the end of the winter solstice and the beginning of longer days. The celebration is incomplete without the traditional Bhangra and Gidda dance performances, which are accompanied by the beats of the dhol, a type of drum.

NIIT celebrated the festival by gathering around a bonfire, singing and shaking legs to traditional folk songs, and feasting on rewri, gajjak, and jaggery delicacies. The celebration started with the lighting of the bonfire in the evening. Usually, families gather around the fire and offer prayers to the deities for a good harvest, prosperity, and happiness. They also throw popcorn, sesame seeds, peanuts, and other offerings into the fire. The celebration started as the sun set and continued till 9. The glittery lights and starry night set a perfect environment to celebrate the festival.

Show your Talent

The saying goes, "Your greatest talent is so much more powerful than your greatest fear."

NIIT university is the one, which can provide plenty of such opportunities where you can showcase your talents. The genius of the plan is in its design, which sets aside specific time for new students to get to know the campus and their fellow classmates.

Students with young minds and developing talents, such as dancing, lyricists, poetry, instrumentalists, fashion walk, singing, acting, storytelling, photography, and mimicry, polish their skills in preparation for an evening performance in front of their co-NU family members. NIIT university is the epicenter for fostering the development of new talent.
